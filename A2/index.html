Biography

Tagore modernised Bengali art by spurning rigid classical forms and resisting linguistic strictures. His novels, stories, songs, dance-dramas, and essays spoke to topics political and personal. Gitanjali (Song Offerings), Gora (Fair-Faced) and Ghare-Baire (The Home and the World) are his best-known works, and his verse, short stories, and novels were acclaimed—or panned—for their lyricism, colloquialism, naturalism, and unnatural contemplation. reshaped Bengali literature and music as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

"I slept and dreamt that life was joy. I awoke and saw that life was service. I acted and behold, service was joy"
Birthday 7 May 1861
Birthplace Kolkata, India
occupation writer
